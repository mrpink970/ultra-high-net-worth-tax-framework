Ultra-High Net Worth Tax Base Integrity Framework

Overview

This repository contains a draft policy framework and supporting documents exploring how the U.S. tax system interacts with extreme wealth accumulation, particularly at the scale of tens or hundreds of billions of dollars.

The work here is not a finished solution, not a political campaign, and not a claim of expertise. It is an attempt to assemble a coherent, good-faith framework that addresses structural features of the tax system that allow very large fortunes to compound indefinitely with limited interaction with the tax base.

The documents are offered as a starting point for discussion, critique, and refinement.

⸻

Why This Exists

This project began with a simple question:

Why does extreme wealth appear to compound faster than the rest of the economy, even when little is sold or taxed?

Exploring that question led to learning about mechanisms that are largely invisible outside tax and estate planning circles, including:
	•	prolonged deferral of unrealized gains,
	•	borrowing against appreciated assets as a substitute for income,
	•	trust structures that allow economic access without realization,
	•	payment-in-kind (PIK) arrangements,
	•	and basis elimination at death.

Prior to this work, I was not aware of many of these mechanisms. This framework emerged from learning about how they function in practice and asking whether the system still aligns with its original intent at extreme scale.

⸻

What This Is
	•	A framework, not a final proposal
	•	A set of draft documents, including one draft bill
	•	An attempt to slow the rate of extreme wealth concentration, not eliminate wealth
	•	A modular approach that separates:
	•	timing of taxation,
	•	rate structure,
	•	and policy objectives

The central idea is to price prolonged deferral and economic access, rather than impose hard caps or a traditional annual wealth tax.

⸻

What This Is Not
	•	Not a wealth tax
	•	Not a call to confiscate assets or force sales
	•	Not a statement that wealth itself is illegitimate
	•	Not a claim that this framework is complete, optimal, or authoritative
	•	Not written by a tax professional or intended to replace expert analysis

⸻

Core Objectives

The framework is guided by a small number of objectives:
	1.	Restore alignment between economic access and tax contribution at extreme scale
	2.	Neutralize indefinite deferral as a dominant long-term strategy
	3.	Preserve ordinary investment incentives for the vast majority of taxpayers
	4.	Reduce reliance on borrowing against appreciated assets as a tax substitute
	5.	Prevent permanent removal of gains from the tax base through timing or death
	6.	Slow the rate of extreme wealth concentration without imposing caps or confiscation
	7.	Ensure preferential capital gains rates do not apply indefinitely at extraordinary realization levels

Contents of This Repository
	•	Draft Bill (v4)
A draft legislative text focused on deferral, borrowing neutrality, aggregation, and basis coordination.
	•	Objectives & Rationale Letter
A high-level explanation of the policy goals and design philosophy, written for legislative staff and policymakers.
	•	Progressive Capital Gains Companion Proposal
A separate, non-legislative proposal outlining progressive capital gains rate adjustments at ultra-large realization levels.

Each document is intentionally scoped and modular. They can be evaluated independently or together.

Invitation for Feedback

This framework is intentionally shared to be improved upon.

Feedback is especially welcome on:
	•	constitutional considerations,
	•	administrability,
	•	unintended behavioral consequences,
	•	interactions with existing tax law,
	•	alternative design choices that better achieve the stated objectives.

Critique is invited in good faith. Refinement is the goal.

⸻

Status

This is an early-stage, public framework.
No endorsement is implied. No legislative outcome is assumed.

If nothing else, the hope is that this contributes constructively to ongoing discussions about inequality, tax design, and how public systems interact with extreme private scale.
